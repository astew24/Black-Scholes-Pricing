    Context

    How to Read the Output

    This demo favors interpretability over market-data complexity.

    Black-Scholes is most useful as a baseline: it prices European options under lognormal returns, constant volatility, and frictionless markets. Real markets break those assumptions, especially around dividends, jumps, skew, and early exercise.

    That is why the charting here is deterministic and client-side. The point is to understand the shape of the model and its sensitivities, not to imply that a single closed-form value is the market truth.
